How We Work

Our BIM Process

A structured, milestone-driven workflow that takes your project from brief to a fully coordinated, construction-ready BIM deliverable.

01

Brief & Scope

We review your project documents, set the BIM Execution Plan (BEP), and agree LOD requirements and deliverable schedule.

02

3D Modelling

Discipline models are built in Revit — Architectural, Structural, and MEP — to agreed LOD and parametric standards.

03

Clash Detection

Federated model reviewed in Navisworks. Hard, soft, and workflow clashes logged in BCF and resolved with discipline leads.

04

Coordination

MEP routing optimised, structural penetrations agreed, and all building systems spatially coordinated across disciplines.

05

Deliverables

Construction drawings, quantity schedules, 4D/5D simulations, and clash-free IFC/RVT models issued via ACC or agreed platform.

06

As-Built & Handover

Models updated to as-built condition, COBie data populated, and a complete digital asset record handed over for facilities management.

Common Questions About BIM

Whether you're new to BIM or evaluating consultants, here are answers to the questions we hear most from architects, contractors, and developers across the UAE and GCC.

LOD (Level of Development) defines how much detail and information a BIM element contains — ranging from LOD 100 (conceptual massing) to LOD 500 (as-built, with real-world tolerances). Most UAE/GCC authorities and contractors work to LOD 300–400 for construction. We'll recommend the right LOD for your project phase during our initial brief.

Yes — we routinely start from 2D AutoCAD drawings, PDFs, and even scanned as-built drawings. Our team traces, interprets, and builds accurate 3D Revit models from whatever documentation you have. For existing buildings, we also offer Scan to BIM using point cloud data.

Timeline depends on project size, complexity, and how quickly design information is available. A mid-size floor plate (2,000–4,000 m²) typically takes 3–6 weeks from information receipt to a clash-free federated model. Yes. We work in alignment with Dubai Municipality BIM requirements, Abu Dhabi's Digital Twin Initiative, and Saudi Vision 2030 project standards. Our BIM Execution Plans (BEPs) are structured to meet authority submission requirements across all major UAE and KSA jurisdictions.

We deliver native Revit (.RVT), federated Navisworks (.NWD/.NWF), IFC (Open BIM), DWG, and PDF. Model sharing is via Autodesk Construction Cloud (ACC/BIM 360) or your preferred CDE. We can also produce custom exports for Archibus, Maximo, or other FM platforms.

All clashes are identified in Navisworks Manage and exported as BCF (BIM Collaboration Format) reports. We categorise by discipline, severity, and location — then host coordination meetings to resolve each item. A clash matrix is maintained throughout the project until all critical clashes reach zero.
